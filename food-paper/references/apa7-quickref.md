# APA 7.0 Quick Reference (default style)

Canonical APA 7 reference for the suite. `food-deep-research`, `food-review`, and
`food-paper` default to APA 7.0 unless a target journal sets another style (via
`journal-selector`). When switching styles, re-flow **both** the reference list
and every in-text marker — never leave a mixed style.

## In-text citation
- Parenthetical: `(Author & Author, 2023)`; three+ authors: `(Author et al., 2023)`.
- Narrative: `Author and Author (2023) reported…`.
- Direct quote: add page/locator — `(Author, 2023, p. 14)`.
- Multiple works: alphabetical, separated by semicolons — `(A, 2021; B, 2023)`.

## Reference list (alphabetical, hanging indent)
- **Journal article:** Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of the article. *Journal Name in Italics*, *Volume*(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxx
  > Zhang, P., & Smith, J. (2023). Antioxidant capacity of fermented soy. *Food Chemistry*, *400*, 133912. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.foodchem.2023.133912
- **Book:** Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work* (edition). Publisher.
- **Book chapter:** Author, A. A. (Year). Chapter title. In E. Editor (Ed.), *Book title* (pp. xx–xx). Publisher.
- **Report/standard (e.g. AOAC, EFSA, Codex):** Organization. (Year). *Title* (Report No. if any). Publisher/URL.
- **Dataset:** Author/Org. (Year). *Title* [Data set]. Repository. https://doi.org/xxxx

## Rules
- Up to 20 authors listed; 21+ → first 19, ellipsis, last author.
- Include the DOI as a URL for everything that has one.
- Title case only for the *journal name*; sentence case for article/book titles.
- Et al. for three or more authors in every in-text citation.
- Every in-text citation must appear in the list, and vice versa (no orphans).
