# Scientific Writing Style (food & nutrition)

Used by `draft_writer` and `polisher`.

## Voice & tense
- Past tense for what was done and found (Methods, Results); present tense for
  established facts and what the data show ("Firmness decreased…"; "Anthocyanins
  are heat-labile").
- Active voice where the journal allows ("We measured…"); passive is acceptable
  in Methods.
- First person plural sparingly; let the science lead.

## Sentences & paragraphs
- One idea per sentence; put the subject early; cut filler ("it is worth noting
  that", "in order to" → "to").
- One point per paragraph, opened by a topic sentence; connect with logical
  transitions (however, therefore, in contrast).
- Prefer concrete quantitative statements over vague ones ("increased by 23%" not
  "increased considerably").

## Terminology & units
- One term per concept, used consistently; define abbreviations once.
- SI units; g/100 g with basis; log CFU/g; report mean ± SD/SEM with n.
- Correct nomenclature (compounds, enzymes with EC numbers where relevant,
  microorganisms in italics).

## Titles & introductions
- **Title:** specific and informative — the system, the factor, and the outcome
  ("Effect of high-pressure processing on anthocyanin retention in blueberry
  purée"); avoid "Study of…", avoid over-claiming.
- **Introduction funnel:** broad importance → what's known → the specific gap →
  the objective/hypothesis; end on the aim. No results in the introduction.

## Discussion
- Interpret, don't restate Results; compare with mechanism and literature (agree/
  disagree, why); state limitations honestly (matrix, dose, n, single batch); end
  with implications proportionate to the evidence. No overreach.
