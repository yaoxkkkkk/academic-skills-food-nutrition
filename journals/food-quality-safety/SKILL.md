# Food Quality and Safety (OUP) — Author Guideline Skill

**Publisher:** Oxford University Press · **ISSN:** 2399-1399 · **Source:** [Instructions to authors](https://academic.oup.com/fqs/pages/General_Instructions) · **Verified:** 2026-07 (confirm at source). Gold open access.

## Aims & scope
Original research and reviews on food quality and safety: quality attributes, safety/risk, detection and analysis, processing effects on quality/safety, and related nutrition and health. Interdisciplinary, application-oriented work welcome.

## Article types
Original Articles; Reviews; Short Communications; Perspectives/Opinion.

## Manuscript structure
Title page → Abstract → Keywords → Introduction → Materials and Methods → Results → Discussion (or combined) → Conclusion → Funding → Conflict of Interest → Data Availability → References → Tables → Figure legends → Figures.

- **Abstract:** unstructured, ≤250 words.
- **Keywords:** 4–6.

## Reference style
**Numbered** citation-sequence style (confirm current OUP style for the journal). In-text bracketed/superscript numbers; numbered list with article titles and DOIs.

Example:
> 1. Author AB, Author CD. Title of the article. Food Quality and Safety. 2023;7:fyadxxx. https://doi.org/10.1093/fqsafe/fyadxxx

## Figures & tables
Figures ≥300 dpi (line art higher); TIFF/EPS/PDF; RGB online; editable tables; cite in order. OUP production redraws/relabels as needed — provide legible source.

## Submission checklist
- [ ] Abstract ≤250 words · [ ] 4–6 keywords · [ ] Numbered references with DOIs
- [ ] Funding + Conflict of Interest + Data Availability statements
- [ ] Ethics/consent statements where applicable · [ ] Figures ≥300 dpi · [ ] Cover letter
